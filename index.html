Semester                Abroad

The semester abroad program(SAP) offered by BITS Pilani is aimed at providing students the right international exposure to ensure wholistic education in every sense of the term. The program allows students to spend a semester in a foreign university having an MoU with BITS or any other university ranked within top 400 universities by the QS or THE ranking systems. To apply for SAP, a student must have completed all CDCs of his/her respective branch before the semester they wish to spend in a foreign university.

The SAP application in the foreign university of choice is the responsibility of the interested student. BITS allows the semester abroad program subject to a student’s acceptance in the respective University’s program. To apply for the program, the student will have to fill and submit the application form for SAP available on the IPCD website. The form requires the students to fill the details of the university they wish to spend their semester at, the courses they are going to take up in the university and the corresponding equivalent courses offered at BITS.

To confirm the equivalent courses offered at BITS, a prescribed form can be filled and submitted to HODs who will approve the equivalence of the courses. The filled application form will then be sent to AUGSD (first degree) or AGSRD (higher degree) for approval. After approval, information is sent to the partner university. On completion of the semester abroad, the credits of the courses completed will be transferred from the partner university to BITS and added to the student’s academic record.

The application form has to be submitted to IPCD Office (Room No.C-228) on or before the last day of classwork of the semester, before SAP semester (usually 30th Nov and 30th Apr). As many as 56 students took an off-campus thesis and completed their thesis work from some of the best universities like NUS Singapore, Max Plank Institute Germany and IISC Bangalore in the second semester of the academic year of 2019-20.

International students at the campus

BITS Pilani is a prestigious institute which has grown its reputation over the years. It ranks 1st among the private engineering institutes in India for the courses of Mechanical and Computer Science Engineering according to the QS rankings.

BITS Pilani does not have a foreign student quota and takes admission of foreign citizens based only on merit. Every year BITS Pilani takes around 25 to 30 international students based on their SAT scores. The institute also provides merit-based scholarship to internationally admitted students for each semester of up to 80% tuition fee waiver. This scholarship is given based on CGPA secured by the student.

The complete information about the process of admission, eligibility and scholarships can be found on
http://sites.bits-hyderabad.ac.in/internationalstuadm/index.php

Research               Collaboration

BITS Pilani aims to collaborate with other institutions around the world to exchange knowledge and information. Keeping this spirit in mind professors at BPPC collaborated with some of the premier institutions globally on various projects. These projects tackled various domains, from resource management to micro-biology, all serving the sole purpose of creating a better society for tomorrow.

University of Queensland :

Dr. Markus Barth and Dr. Kai-Hsiang Chuang - leaders in advanced Magnetic Resonance Imaging, offer students pursuing masters in neuroscience, biophysics, biomedical, bioengineering, bioscience or other relevant scientific discipline to collaborate with them on Functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI) on rodents without anaesthesia. This study will serve as a first step for larger projects, which will involve evaluation of learning, memory and experimental therapeutics in animal models and translation to humans.

ThinkSwiss Research Scholarships:

Undergraduate or Masters students who completed 2nd year are eligible to take advantage of this scholarship, funded by the Swissnex Network (part of the Swiss State Secretariat for Education, Research and Innovation) if they have a written confirmation from a Professor at a Swiss university.

Mitacs Globalink Research Internship :

The Mitacs Globalink Research Internship is a competitive initiative for international undergraduates. Top-ranked applicants participate in a 12-week research internship under the supervision of Canadian university faculty members in a variety of academic disciplines, from science, engineering and mathematics to the humanities and social sciences. The opportunity is up for grabs to any BITSian interested in research in the top universities of Canada.

Okinawa Institute Research Internship Program :

The Okinawa Institute of Science and Technology offers education and research opportunities to graduate and undergraduate students under their esteemed professors. Living and other necessary facilities including an allowance are also given under the program.

Max Planck Institute for Software Systems:

Max Planck Institute for Software Systems is one of the most prestigious institutions in the world offers internships to students of Computer Science and IT backgrounds at various levels of their education and a chance to pursue research in various CS fields under the guidance of their expert faculty.

Université de Lausanne :

Université de Lausanne, Switzerland provides students, currently enrolled in Bachelor’s in Biological Sciences a chance to conduct research in various areas of the subject at their world class labs during the course of their semester.

S.N. Bose Scholar’s Program :

S.N. Bose Scholar’s Program is a collaborative effort of the Science & Engineering Board (SERB), Department of Science and Technology (DST), Govt. of India, the Indo-U.S. Science and Technology Forum (IUSSTF) and WINStep Forward. This program aims to inculcate a passion for research, present it as a viable career option and provide an opportunity to Indian students to experience world-class research facilities in leading U.S. institutions. This scholarship is awarded to the students studying in various prestigious colleges in India. BITSians studying in the various disciplines offered can grab this scholarship and pursue research in their field of interest.

Khorana Program for Scholars

The Department of Biotechnology (DBT), Govt. of India, Indo-U.S. Science and Technology Forum (IUSSTF) and WINStep Forward are partnering to support the prestigious Khorana Program for Scholars. The program will provide opportunities to Indian students to undertake research at University of Wisconsin-Madison (UW) and partner universities in Summer 2020 for a period of 10-12 weeks. This initiative aims to provide encouragement to young scholars to undertake R&D at premier Universities in the United States. This scholarship is awarded to the students studying in various prestigious colleges in India. BITSians enrolled in Biology and Pharmaceutical backgrounds can grab this scholarship.

Kaohsiung Medical University, Taiwan

KMU Chemistry offers more than 25 fully-funded short-term research internship (2020) positions for the partner universities/Institutions in India. Students enrolled in PhD and MSc programs at prestigious schools are given this opportunity.

Hiroshima University

Hiroshima University through its International Linkage Degree Program (ILDP) has collaborated with BITS to provide internships at various projects going on in the research labs of HU.
